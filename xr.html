Mixed Reality

Mixed reality (MR) is the merging of real and virtual worlds to produce new environments and visualizations, where physical and digital objects co-exist and interact in real time. - Wikipedia

In my work I tried to use available technology to produce simple MR scenes and test different concepts of how we could use this technology in the current stadium.

Technology used

To realize my visions I used a variety of tools. Beginning with the HMD (head mounted display), which is a Lenovo Mirage Solo based on Googles Daydream platform. This HMD was released in 2018 and was unfortunately not really successfull. Google eventually decided to no longer develop the Daydream ecosystem and therefore cancelling support.

Yet all resources to Daydream are still online and the SDKs are still usable in Unity 2018/partly 2019.

Blender

Used to create, texturize and optimize most of the assets.

Unity

As the game engine, Unity is the heart of every app I make.

GVR SDK

An SDK created by Google which contains all the XR related scripts.

Daydream Elements

Contains a library of useful examples and prefabs to use with Unity.

The Museum

The Museum was one of the first real applications I implemented after playing around for a long time. It shows a statue placed in any real room.

The tinted black and white image is the pass through image from the Mirage Solo. In the early stages I really disliked the tone map, but came to terms with it. In a way this really helps to focus on the object.

What really surprised me are the tracking capabilities in an augmented scene. There is very little drifting in a well lit room and the objects stay almost perfectly in their spot.
